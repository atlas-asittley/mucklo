# Realm of Atlas — Content Creation Guide

## Rules for Adding New Areas

When adding new areas, cities, dungeons, or any explorable location to the game:

1. **Unique sprites** — every enemy type gets its own drawn sprite. No emoji. No duplicate sprites across enemy types.

2. **Distinct theme** — each area has a unique visual identity, color palette, atmosphere, and lore. It should feel different from all other areas.

3. **Enemy variety** — at least 3-5 enemy types, each visually and mechanically distinct.

4. **Items that fit the area** — shops and drops match the theme. Fire area = fire gear. Ice area = ice gear. etc.

5. **At least one NPC** — each area has personality, not just enemies. NPCs give the area character.

6. **World map integration** — new areas connect to the existing map via paths or markers. Players know how to get there.

7. **No copy-paste content** — each area should feel genuinely different, not a reskin. Unique tile sets, unique NPCs, unique atmosphere.

8. **Meaningful rewards** — gear or items from the area should be worth the risk. Bosses drop specific legendary items.

9. **Secret areas or hidden content** — bonus discovery inside the area makes exploration rewarding.

## Enemy Design
- Every enemy type gets a unique drawn sprite
- Each enemy has a name and personality (not just "Fire Goblin 1")
- Enemy difficulty should match the area's theme
- At least one boss-type enemy per major area

## NPC Design
- NPCs have personality and dialog
- At least one shop per area
- NPCs should react to the player's class or level
